news

06/2026
Our paper "Health-informed computing" is accepted by Communications of the ACM. The paper estimates that U.S. data centers' annual public health costs could exceed $20 billion by 2028 and proposes a health-informed computing framework to mitigate these impacts across space and time.
07/2026
Our review article "Strategies and design for increasing AI sustainability" is published in Nature Reviews Clean Technology. The paper examines the growing environmental impacts of AI data centers and highlights strategies such as hardware design, grid-integrated operation, and joint energy-water-carbon management to increase AI sustainability.
09/2025
Our paper "Fairness-Regularized Online Optimization with Switching Costs" is accepted by NeurIPS'25. This paper studies robust online decison-making with long-term fairness consideration and is motivated by many real-world applications such as balancing the public health impact of AI data centers across different regions.
09/2025
Our guest article "The Real Story on AI's Water Use" is published in IEEE Spectrum and clarifies some key facts of AI's water footprint to facilitate evidence-based public discussions. A longer version is published in OECD AI Policy Observatory.
02/2025
My TED talk is live now!
09/2024
I'm glad to share four recent papers accepted by SIGMETRICS'25 and NeurIPS'24. (1) The SIGMETRICS'25 paper "Learning-Augmented Decentralized Online Convex Optimization in Networks" proposes a novel algorithm to provably robustify machine learning predictions for decentralized optimization in networks. (2) The NeurIPS'24 paper "Bileve: Securing Text Provenance in Large Language Models Against Spoofing with Bi-level Signature" proposes a novel anti-spoofing watermarking method to safeguard LLM-generated content. (3) The NeurIPS'24 paper "Online Budgeted Matching with General Bids" proposes provably-competitive online algorithms for budgeted matching with arbitrary bid sizes. (4) The NeurIPS'24 paper "Safe Exploitative Play in Stochastic Bayesian Games with Untrusted Type Beliefs" quantifies the impact of potentially incorrect type beliefs on an agent's payoff in Bayesian games.
